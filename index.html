AFROTC Dictionary

Introduction

The purpose of this dictionary is to provide individuals new to AFROTC a general base of terminology often used by members of the program. It is also for friends & family of students in the program who wish to better understand what these terms mean. All of the information found here is typically learned within the first semester that a student joins. Some of the provided information is specific to the University of Hawaii at Manoa AFROTC program, and may not be applicable to other schools.

Air Force Reserve Officer Training Corps (AFROTC)

This is a college program which allows students to earn a degree, and commission as a 2nd Lieutenant in the Air Force after graduation. Students must take Aerospace Studies classes, attend LLAB participate in PT, and complete Field Training in the summer after their AS 200 year.

Aerospace Studies Year (AS Year)

An indication of a cadet's level and how long they have been in the program.

Freshman: AS 100
Sophomore: AS 200 / AS 250
Junior: AS 300
Senior: AS 400
5-year degree: AS 700

If a student joins in their Sophomore year, they will be an AS 250, where they must take both AS 100, and AS 200 classes simultaneously.

Cadet

A student who is in the AFROTC program, taking Aerospace Studies classes, PT and LLAB. While at AFROTC related functions, students refer to each other by last name by saying, "Cadet LastName". This applies to both GMC and POC cadets. Cadre also refer to AFROTC students with the same naming convention.

Cadre

The staff responsible for operating a detachment, and the training of all GMC and POC cadets. Cadre are Air Officers and Enlisted members assigned to a detachment. There may also be civilian Cadre who work directly for the school. The number of Cadre assigned depend on the size of the detachment. There is a Detachment Commander who is a Lieutenant Colonel or Colonel. Other officers are either Captains or Majors. They teach the Aerospace Studies classes. Enlisted Cadre are typically Staff or Technical Sergeants. They run the administrative side of the program.

Chain of Command

This is a hierarchical system in which every cadet has an immediate supervisor who is also a cadet. A "chain" refers to a sequence of multiple cadets that are linked together. This can be viewed through a Wing Organization Chart. Some cadets may have multiple subordinates, but no cadet has more than two immediate supervisors. When information and tasks and given, it is disseminated from the top-down. When something is requested, it is "passed up" the chain. When a problem arises, cadets are highly encouraged to go through their immediate supervisor for help, rather than "jumping the chain" and asking someone higher up. This is to minimize the number of questions and problems for those higher up in the chain-of-command.

Customs & Courtesies

Cultural expectations of Air Force members and cadets. This includes when and how to salute officers, what to do in different scenarios involving the Unites States Flag, and how to act in various professional and social settings. Cadets learn proper customs & courtesies in their AS 100 year, and are expected to use it appropriately for the rest of their time as a cadet.
For for information, look up Air Force Instruction 34-1201.

Drill & Ceremonies (D&C)

Drill refers to the various formations and marching movements that cadets use to move from one location to another. Marching is moving while following explicitly described rules which define what and how a cadet is allowed to move. The purpose of using drill is to provides a means to evaluate a cadet's attention to detail, and decision making when in command.
Ceremonies refers to formal events such as parades, and is an extension of drill. Ceremonies are performed to recognize and honor individuals, and for special events.
For more information, look up Air Force Instruction 36-2203.

Dress & Appearance

Grooming standards put in place for all individuals in the Air Force. It prescribes the maximum length for hair, shaving rules, uniform dress code, and use of accessories while in an Air Force uniform. The purpose of having standards is to present a shared professional image. All cadets are expected to comply with Dress & Appearance standards that can be found in Air Force Instruction 36-2903.

Field Training

This is a training camp which all GMC cadets must attend after their AS 200 year if they wish to become a POC cadet. It typically lasts between 3-4 weeks. Cadets will apply all they have learned in their GMC years. It is designed to test a cadet's leadership capabilities while in a stressed environment. It is split into two portions: InGarrison (InG) and Air Expeditionary Force (AEF). InG focuses on Drill & Ceremonies and Custom & Courtesies , while AEF focuses more on dealing with an austere environment.

Flight

A type of drill formation with at least five cadets. One cadet is denoted as the flight commander, and is in charge of the overall operation and safety of the cadets. GMC cadets are assigned a flight for the duration of an entire semester, where they will be given various positions, do [PT]PT, and attend LLAB] with. There are typically 1-2 POC cadets who directly supervise and lead each flight. During the Fall Semester, AS 100 and 200 cadets mix together, as opposed to Spring FTP Semester.

Field Training Preparation Semester (FTP Semester)

The semester for AS 200 cadets which precedes Field Training. Flights are created based on AS year, splitting up the AS 200s and 100s. The focus for AS 200s shifts from training and development to evaluation under high stress conditions. The goal of FTP is to give AS 200 cadets experience making decisions and performing while under pressure, and determine who is eligible to attend Field Training.

Group Leadership Project (GLP)

A challenge designed to evaluate the leadership capabilities of a single cadet. The cadet in charge of the GLP must effectively lead his/her team in accomplishing a specified task. GLPs may have specific rules and guidelines which the entire team must follow, which would otherwise result in a penalty of some sort. There is an evaluator who will fill out a LCE form on the cadet, and enforce the rules of the GLP.

General Military Course Cadet (GMC Cadet)

These are lower division cadets, typically in their first or second year of the program. Each GMC cadet is placed into a flight for the duration of a semester. They will be given various positions within the flight where they will be evaluated on their performance by POC cadets. The goal of the GMC years is to learn basic military Customs & Courtesies, develop their leadership, and prepare for FTP semester.

Leadership Competency Evaluation (LCE)

A form used to quantify the effectiveness of a cadet's leadership abilities in a given position. It lists several qualities which can be awarded 0-2 points, with a max score of 27. The main sections of a LCE are, "Mission Development", "Mission Planning", and "Mission Execution". One important note is that task completion is worth 1 point, meaning it is possible to fail the given task exceed standards.

Leadership Laboratory (LLAB)

This is a 2-hour class that is planned and run by the POC cadets, once a week. Various objectives outlined by HQAFROTC are met. A typical LLAB may consist of briefings on miscellaneous topics, drill practice for a new marching command, and/or GLPs.

Official Memorandum (OM)

An official document to communicate or make requests with another group or individual. There are specific guidelines which standardize the way a memorandum is written. Cadets may be assigned to write memorandums, or they may write memorandums to ask for something specific and explain why. For more information on how to write an OM, look up Tongue and Quil.

Professional Officer Course Cadet (POC Cadet)

These are upper division cadets who have completed Field Training. They have an AS Year of 300 or higher. The focus for POC cadets is developing their leadership capabilities in various scenarios. POC cadets are in charge of directly training and developing the GMC cadets. GMC cadets must salute and render proper greetings to POC cadets. All POC cadets have a newly assigned position for the duration of a given semester.
See Wing Organization Chart

Physical Training (PT)

These are workout sessions involving the entire Cadet Wing. The Physical Fitness Officers plan and execute PT twice a week for 1-hour periods. The goal of PT is to help improve the fitness level of cadets, provide a baseline for what will be expected at Field Training, and prepare cadets for the Physical Fitness Assessment.

Warrior Knowledge

The information that all cadets are required to know for LLAB. It consists things such as rank structures, mission statements, the Air Force Song, quotes, and other Air Force related knowledge. This information must be memorized verbatim, and cadets must be able to recall it when asked to do so. There is a handbook which notes what new information must be learned each week. Wing Organizational Chart (Org Chart)

A diagram which shows the chain of command and positions for POC cadets. It is created by the Cadet Wing Commander, which is the highest-ranking cadet position. A new Wing Organization Chart is created each semester and all POC have a new position associated with it. Some positions are leadership roles over GMC cadets, such as Flight Commanders, and leadership over other POC cadets, such as the Wing Commander. Some lead specific areas of training like Physical Fitness Officers, and others assist in mission effectiveness, such as the Communications Officer.
